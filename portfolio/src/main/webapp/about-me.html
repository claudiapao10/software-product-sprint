About Me: Claudia Monge

Hello! My name is Claudia Monge Torres. Currently and at the moment, I am a full-time computer science student.
I originated in the town of Humacao, Puerto Rico and continued my studies in Bayamón, where I have been able to have my beginning in university life being a by present in clubs and associations inluding "Women in Cybersecurity - Bayamón Chaper" and the "Computer Science Student Association". These experiences disciplined me and made me know more about my passions within programming.
One topic that I am passionate about is improving the local economy and the growth of small businesses in Puerto Rico. Given this, I started a project called 'De la Casa' with some of my friends in the field of technology to ideate the development of a platform that focuses on this sector of the Puerto Rican economy and give them the spotlight they deserve. I have used all these experiences to believe and discover who I really am, to learn and unlearn so that in the near future I can positively impact the community.
Now that I'm here in Google's Software Product Sprint, I hope to keep growing and learning from such experienced professionals.

Who I am:

How I started

I have always been very curious, even as a kid, about technology. I was able to learn how to use the computer at an early age because I spenta great amount of time at my mom's office supply store. But the key experience that fueled my passion for technology was participating in a coding camp before entering high school. In this camp they taught us the introduction to the fundamentals of programming and gave us a preview of startups that where beggining to form in Puerto Rico. Having the opportunity to meet a large number of people who spoke so passionately about their projects or their work and the great experience that I had together were key to deciding on the career that I am currently in.


How I envision the future

From here to the future I want to be able to experiment in different areas of computer science and choose some to continue my studies. I envision myself during this time being a voice for girls in tech. I want to do a mentoring program in order to encourage the participation of more girls in the STEM area. In this way, it could help to close the gender gap in these areas. As I mentioned earlier, I would like to be able to carry out the project that seeks to improve the local economy and the growth of small businesses in Puerto Rico. This is, since a large part of the Puerto Rican economy falls on small and local businesses. On top of that, I want to be part of the people who empower and inspire other women to be part of the movement to increase the representation of women in STEM. Since there is already knowledge about the gender disparity in STEM, I would like to come up with an initiative or be part of one as this would give girls and women opportunities in the field beforehand.
